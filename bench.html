The Basics of the Bench Press

How to perform a bench press

Bench press is performed with the back and glutes on the bench. The lifter holds the bar and unracks it. The lifter then begins to lower the bar to the chest. Once the bar is motionless on the chest, the lifter lifts the bar back up by extending the arms. Below is a video by PowerliftingToWin who goes into a great detail on how to perform a bench press.

Common causes for disqualification in competition

  • Failure to observe the referee's signals at the commencement or completion of the lift.
  • Any change in the elected position that results in the buttocks breaking contact with the bench or lateral movement of the hands (between the referee's signals).
  • Any excessive movement or change of contact of the feet during the lift proper.
  • Allowing the bar to sink into the chest after receiving the referee's signal.
  • Pronounced uneven extension of the arms during or at the completion of the lift.
  • Any downward motion of the bar during the course of being pressed out.
  • Contact with the bar by the spotters between the referee's signals.
  • Any contact of the lifter's shoes with the bench or its supports.
  • Deliberate contact between the bar and the bar rest uprights during the lift to assist the completion of the press.
